Burger Bach

Nothing better describes the change that Durham has been undergoing better than Burger Bach. As the name implies, this restaurant is a New Zealand inspired gastropub that serves an arsenal of highly delicious and innovative burgers. With these descriptors accompany a price tag that isn’t unreasonable but is certainly higher than what many are willing to pay for a burger.

It is not a big secret that Durham is undergoing an intense process of gentrification in areas historically occupied by African Americans. Exemplifying this urban renewal are the restaurants and businesses that elevate elements of American culture to a level tailored to the new face of Durham: young working professionals (see Boxcar Arcade, Dame’s Chicken and Waffles). This is not to say that Burger Bach is bad, but it seems to be a labor of love. Walking out from feasting on a truly great burger, don’t be surprised if you encounter homelessness on the nearby 9th Street.
