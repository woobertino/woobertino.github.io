Ko An

Though Asian fusion is abundantly found in the Triangle’s food scene, the emphasis on modernizing the aspect of eating at home in Asia is rare. ko•an celebrates Southeast Asian dining most notably through bridging western and eastern approaches towards eating meals. Difficult to put into words, the open dining concept fostered an atmosphere of communal dining that felt oddly monastic.

Interestingly, ko•an approaches food by reminding us of the rustic origins of the many dishes that we are now familiar with in fusion cuisine such as pho, curry, and korean bbq. The tension that exists between simplicity and price was not one to be ignored, and it makes me wonder how these higher-end Asian fusion restaurants are perceived by the street vendor in Vietnam who sells breakfast pho for 50 cents a bowl.
