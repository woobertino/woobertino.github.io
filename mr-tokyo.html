Mr. Tokyo

There’s no need to sugar coat Mr. Tokyo - it’s a popular all-you-can-eat sushi buffet that also serves other small Japanese dishes made to order. The joy of this restaurant is in the different reasons that customers may have for coming. While some want to stuff themselves with endless over-sauced sushi, others look to take advantage of the unlimited hibachi and appetizers that are certainly more delicious than that found in a traditional Chinese buffet.

Being a frequent diner at Mr. Tokyo has made me consider how “buffet culture” in America draws so strongly from Asian influences. Though popular chains, such as Golden Corral, exist, I would argue that the family-owned Chinese buffets outnumber corporate locations by a wide margin in all parts of the country. The desire to get the biggest bang for your buck contrasts so strongly with East Asian values of restraint and shared dining, and in some ways, it establishes an unhealthy relationship with food that has led to obesity being considered an epidemic of the same scale as heart disease.
